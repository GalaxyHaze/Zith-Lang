Welcome to the diary

2026-06-13

This is the first entry in what I hope will become a regular habit — writing down thoughts about the Zith compiler, programming language design, and the day-to-day joys and struggles of building a systems language from scratch.

Right now Zith is at v0.2.0. The lexer, parser, semantic analysis, type system, and MIR lowering are all functional. I've been spending time on the heuristic engine (the "did you mean?" feature) and expanding the test suite. The goal is to reach a point where Zith can compile meaningful programs — maybe even self-host one day.

This blog is just a simple directory of HTML files sitting on the gh-pages branch. No database, no CMS. Lightweight, retro, mine. That's how I like it.

Anyway, that's enough for today. Let's see what comes next.

— Diogo
